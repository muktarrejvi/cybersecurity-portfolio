# Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) - Activation Procedure

## Disaster Declaration

The **first and most critical step** in any Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) is the **formal declaration of a disaster**. 

This declaration allows the organization to shift focus from normal operations to emergency response mode and activates the appropriate recovery procedures.

> **Note:** Every organization's DRP is unique, but most follow a similar sequence of events.

## Standard Disaster Recovery Activation Sequence

Once a disaster is declared, the organization should follow this general sequence:

1. **Declaration of Disaster**  
   Formal announcement that a disaster has occurred and normal operations are suspended.

2. **Activation of the DR Team**  
   Notify and mobilize the Disaster Recovery Team, including key personnel and leadership.

3. **Internal Communications**  
   Establish ongoing internal communication channels (this step continues throughout the recovery process).

4. **Protection of Human Safety**  
   Ensure the safety of all employees, contractors, and visitors (e.g., evacuation, headcount, medical assistance).

5. **Damage Assessment**  
   Conduct a rapid assessment of the impact on facilities, systems, data, and infrastructure.

6. **Execution of System-Specific DRPs**  
   Activate individual Disaster Recovery Plans for critical systems and networks.

7. **Recovery of Mission-Critical Business Processes**  
   Prioritize and restore the most essential business functions first.

8. **Recovery of All Other Business Processes**  
   Restore remaining non-critical systems and operations to normal state.

---

## Key Principles

- **Human Safety First**: Always prioritize life and safety over assets and systems.
- **Clear Declaration Authority**: Only authorized personnel should have the power to declare a disaster.
- **Regular Testing**: This procedure must be tested through tabletop exercises and full simulations.
- **Documentation**: All steps taken during the disaster must be properly logged for post-incident review and insurance purposes.
